The Science

What peptides actually do

Peptides are short chains of amino acids — naturally produced by your body in over 7,000 forms. The ones we use are studied for their ability to signal cells, regulate appetite, and influence metabolic function. They're not stimulants, not steroids, not a crash diet.

🧬

Signal, not force

Peptides like Retatrutide and Tirzepatide engage natural receptors involved in appetite, insulin sensitivity, and energy use. They don't override your biology — they nudge it back into rhythm.

🩺

Why a doctor matters

Self-prescribed peptides ordered online have no oversight, no lab work, and no plan to adjust the dose if something's off. Our physician calibrates the protocol from labs, monitors response, and changes course when needed.

📈

A protocol, not a prescription

Real weight loss is rarely one peptide. We often combine appetite regulators with metabolic and recovery support, and we adjust the stack as your body responds. Weekly check-ins keep the plan accurate.

Foundational

Retatrutide

GIP / GLP-1 / Glucagon Triple Agonist

A newer-generation triple-receptor agonist studied for significant weight reduction and improvements in insulin sensitivity. Often the centerpiece of aggressive medical protocols.

Studied for appetite regulation, glycemic control, and metabolic activation.

Tirzepatide

GIP / GLP-1 Dual Agonist

FDA-approved dual-receptor agonist (Mounjaro). Studied for appetite suppression, glycemic control, and meaningful weight reduction in clinical trials.

Engages two key metabolic receptors at once for compound effect.

Tesamorelin

Growth Hormone Releasing Hormone Analog

Studied for stubborn abdominal fat reduction and improvements in lean body composition. Often layered alongside primary agonists.

Targets visceral fat while supporting lean tissue retention.

MOTS-c

Mitochondrial Peptide

A mitochondrial-derived peptide studied for its role in insulin sensitivity, cellular energy, and exercise capacity — useful when metabolic health is the bottleneck.

Supports cellular energy production and metabolic flexibility.

The doctor will design your specific protocol during the evaluation — including whether to combine peptides for compound effect, and which dosing schedule fits your labs and lifestyle.
